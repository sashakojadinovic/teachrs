What is Chemistry?

An Introduction to Matter, Substances, and How the World Works

Hi Mark, is there any chemistry between us?

Hi Sara, Chemistry is everywhere. In us, and all around us.

Sara: That’s not really what I asked, but go on—I love hearing you talk 😊

Marko: Then listen closely. Chemistry is a wonderful science.

Sara: And what does this wonderful science actually study? I’d love for the object of my admiration to tell me about the object of study in chemistry 😊

Marko: Chemistry studies matter, so we can say that the main focus of chemistry is matter itself.

Sara: And what exactly is matter?

Marko: Matter is anything that takes up space and has mass. There are so many examples that I’m actually struggling to think of something that isn’t made of matter.

Sara: What about love, Marko? Love has no mass and takes up no space, although it feels like butterflies are filling up my stomach right now.

Marko: You’re right. Love is an emotion—it’s not matter. But everything around us, and even we ourselves, are made of matter.

Sara: So you’re saying, for example, that my phone is an object made of different types of matter—like plastic, glass, and metal?

Marko: Exactly. And by the way, there are many types of plastic. I happen to know that your phone is made of a kind of plastic called polycarbonate, and the metal parts are made of aluminum.

Sara: I like how you “just happened” to find out what my phone is made of 😏

Marko: I love knowing everything related to science, Sara.

Sara: You love. That sounds so romantic, Marko.

Marko: Believe it or not, if we explore love scientifically, we’ll end up back in chemistry—there are substances involved in the feeling of being in love.

Sara: So there *is* chemistry between us. I knew it! 😍
